samograph.dev — Product Brief

Zero-setup hosted meeting bot that streams a shareable, persisted live transcript you can read along with — and keep forever.

01Goal & why

Goal. A zero-setup, hosted SaaS that puts a Recall.ai bot in your calls and streams a perfect, shareable, persisted live transcript to a page you (and anyone with the link) can read along with as the call happens — then keeps that transcript forever in your account.

Why it exists

  • The existing CLI samograph works, but it demands Bun, a RECALL_API_KEY, and an ngrok/cloudflared tunnel — out of reach for nearly every non-CLI user.
  • Paid tools (Otter, Fireflies, Read, tl;dv) bury the live transcript and none offers a public shareable live read-along link.
  • Live read-along is the weekly killer use case: strong-accent stand-ups, multilingual customer calls, hard-to-hear audio.
  • Recall.ai keeps video only ~7 days on the free plan, so transcripts must be persisted to our DB in real time — and transcript-first is what we want anyway.

Anti-framing (binding)

NOT a desktop CLI for end users (no token, no tunnel) · NOT a Zoom/Meet plugin (bot joins via the standard link) · NOT an AI-summary product in v1 (the thesis: streaming + persisting a great live transcript already beats every paid tool).

03Architecture at a glance

User → samograph.dev auth → Google Calendar auto-join → one shared Recall token → thin per-call Recall bot → webhook worker → persist to user DB + WebSocket fan-out → shareable /c/<token> read-along page. Billing arrives only in v2.

Single success metric — W2 live-transcript-open ratio. Distinct opens of /c/<token> for owned meetings in week 2 ÷ meetings auto-joined that week. Target ≥ 0.6. Measures whether the read-along differentiator is actually used — not vanity signups.

05v1 user stories

Each ships with a persona, outcome, and manual-test recipe in the spec.

  • U1Sign in with Google in under 30s → dashboard with "Calendar connected" badge.
  • U2Connect Calendar; bot auto-joins the next meeting and the per-call page streams live.
  • U3Read along live and share the link; words appear ≤2s after spoken, "live • N viewers" badge, no PII.
  • U4Custom dictionary (Recall keyterm hints) improves recognition on the next call.
  • U5Post-call: transcript is durable, attributed, exportable to .txt / .md — even after Recall video expires.
  • U6Consent & recording disclosure: bot name says "recording transcript" + auto-posted in-chat disclosure; one-click Leave.
  • U7Per-meeting opt-out (toggle or [private]/[notranscribe] in title) keeps 1:1s private.
  • U8The SaaS promise: tunnel-free, key-free, install-free — all Recall plumbing runs server-side.

Explicit v1 non-goals

No LLM/AI features · no long-term video storage · no MS Teams · no paid plans/seats (single free tier with rate limits) · no branded bots · no post-call email · no native mobile (responsive web only).

06v2 scope — deferred

Listed so v1 architecture leaves the right seams open.

  • V2Synced transcript + video viewer (click a word → video seeks).
  • V2Cheap long-term video storage (R2 / Backblaze B2 cold storage).
  • V2Consistent per-workspace named bot identities.
  • V2Post-call email of transcript to detected participants.
  • V2Multi-language UI (i18n keys laid out in v1).
  • V2MS Teams support.
  • V2Team workspaces, shared libraries, per-seat billing (Stripe).
  • V2Live AI assist (opt-in — the first AI feature).
  • V2Pre-admit consent flow (hold bot in lobby until host admits).
